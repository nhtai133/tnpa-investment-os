# Glossary

## Core Terms

- Account: A container where holdings are tracked, such as a mandate, strategy, sleeve, or custody account.
- Asset: An investable economic exposure, such as a company, fund, bond issuer, currency, commodity, or private asset. Every asset carries exactly one asset class.
- Asset class: The primary classification of an asset. Supported classes are: Stock, Crypto, Cash, Mutual Fund, Private Loan, Opportunity Fund, and Other.
- Instrument: A tradable or trackable security linked to an asset, such as an equity ticker, bond, fund share class, option, or private holding.
- Net Worth Snapshot: A system-generated, point-in-time record of total portfolio value and per-asset-class breakdown. Used to power the Net Worth Command Center dashboard.
- Net Worth Command Center: The primary dashboard surface showing total net worth, asset allocation, asset class summaries, top holdings, recent decisions, watchlist, and rebalance alerts.
- Portfolio: A managed collection of accounts, positions, cash, and allocation targets.
- Position: A holding in an instrument within an account or portfolio.
- Target allocation: The desired weight for an asset class or instrument within a portfolio, expressed as a percentage of total portfolio value with lower and upper tolerance bands.
- Rebalance alert: A system-detected condition where an asset class or position has drifted outside its target allocation bands, requiring attention.
- Thesis: A versioned investment view that states the reason to own, avoid, monitor, add, trim, or exit an exposure.
- Decision record: A formal record of an investment action or non-action, including rationale, owner, status, and approval evidence.
- Catalyst: A future event or condition expected to affect the investment case.
- Risk: A condition that could invalidate, impair, or materially change the thesis.
- Watchlist: A curated set of assets or instruments under active observation, optionally with alert flags and review dates.
- Review cycle: A scheduled or event-driven process for reassessing a thesis, position, or portfolio.

## Data Classification

- Source data: Data imported or entered from an external source.
- Derived data: Data calculated by the system from source data.
- Judgment data: User-authored analysis, assumptions, ratings, and decisions.
- Audit data: Immutable records of who changed what, when, and why where applicable.
